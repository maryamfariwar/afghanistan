Balkh Province is one of the 34 provinces of Afghanistan,
located in the northern part of the country. It's one of Afghanistan's most historically significant regions and has long been a cultural and economic center.
Balkh city (now a small town near Mazar-i-Sharif) was once known as Bactra, the capital of ancient Bactria, a prominent center in the Persian and Hellenistic worlds. It was a major hub for Zoroastrianism, Buddhism, and later, Islamic scholarship. Known as the "Mother of Cities" (Umm al-Bilad) due to its historical significance. Destroyed by the Mongols in the 13th century but later rebuilt.
Located in northern Afghanistan, bordering Uzbekistan to the north. Shares internal borders with Samangan, Jowzjan, Sar-e Pol, and Baghlan provinces. The Amu Darya river forms part of the northern boundary. Features both mountainous terrain and fertile plains.

      Capital: Mazar-i-Sharif
      Area: ~16,840 square kilometers
      Population: Approx. 1.6 to 1.7 million (as of recent estimates)
      Ethnic Groups: Predominantly Tajiks, Uzbeks, Hazaras, Pashtuns, Turkmen, and Arabs
      Languages: Dari (Persian), Uzbek, Pashto, Turkmen
